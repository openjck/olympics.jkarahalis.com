EDU 7701: Research and Development in Innovative Instruction

Summer 2009
Dr. Frank Smith

This course presented by Dr. Frank Smith began with a review of the Constructivist model in determining the frame of reference we are using in order to evaluate teachers’ work and the kind of work a student is using that can be judged via this model. Dr Smith offered my cohort members and I the opportunity to review a unique educational program entitled “High Tech High”. In such a program it became apparent that teachers at HTH must be willing to abandon most of the “traditional” training and conventional ideas they had been told of what makes a superior teacher. From the principle down, staff at HTH must be willing to step outside the box of traditional teaching and explore their creative ideas and talents with their students. The testimonials and videos gave us an exhilarating observation of how such a program can be successful and how one would go about attempting such a program from the ones have been accustomed to within our districts.

In order to share ideas and thoughts on the H.T.H program members of my cohort and me were broken up into four groups to begin the process of using a new program “Moodle”, a course management system” where we would begin by performing the roles of students with Dr. Smith as teacher to gain first hand experience with this new technology. The assignment served two main purposes. First it gave us an opportunity to share our feelings within our subgroup and to present our findings to the other groups, and second it enabled us to gain experience operating a new software program relevant to our roles in school.

The second part of the course had cohort members take on the roles of teachers in using Moodle in creating a matrix and to produce a lesson plan utilizing this new web technology. Within our assigned groups cohort members were able to generate professional looking programs that were presented to the cohort and Dr. Smith. Learning this new technology has given our cohort a new and innovative tool that can be utilized not only from our present roles as school personnel but as future leaders.
