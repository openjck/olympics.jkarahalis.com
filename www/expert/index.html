I consider myself rather fortunate to be able to peruse my education as I work in the field of Psychology. My experiences include working as a School Psychologist for the last 15 years with emotionally disturbed elementary through high school students. In this setting I have been able to accurately classify and therapeutically remediate students with severe emotional and learning problems. My position involves conducting psychological reevaluations that involve both cognitive and projective testing. I also meet with teachers to devise and implement appropriate behavioral programs to assist in the successes of the students I service. On some occasions I am involved in crises situations that may involve students in severe aggressive outbursts that may become self-injurious or destructive. 

My secondary career is as an Adjunct Professor of Psychology at two local colleges, Suffolk Co. Community College and St. Joseph's College. As an Adjunct Professor I teach both young adults and older students courses in Psychology from introduction to advanced I have with continued enthusiasm been teaching at these programs for 20 and 8 years respectively. In this capacity I feel I can use my years of real life experience in presenting the class materials to my students and light a spark in their wonder of the human Psyche.
