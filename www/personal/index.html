Personal

"Acquired knowledge, like wealth benefits only the recipient if not utilized and passed onto benefit the common good" 

                                                                       J. Karahalis

 ____________________________________________________________________________________ 

My name is John Karahalis. I am the proud father of three children, two in college and my youngest in elementary school. I have been actively involved in the field of education and psychology with experience working for over 25 years with  children, adolescents and adults with a multitude of challenges including physical disabilities, mental illness, emotional and learning handicaps.  

I began my career with humble but valuable experiences as a Mental Hygiene Therapy Aid, (M.H.T.A.) for the Office of Mental Retardation and Developmental Disabilities, (O.M.R.D.D.).  As an undergraduate Psychology major my role was to assist and teach severely and profoundly mentally retarded group home residents in activities of daily living.  Included were all aspects of self-help skills including dressing, bathing and toileting.

I later was offered the position of Assistant Manager of another group home for a non-profit agency where my role became more of a supervisory role as well as assisting staff performing the same duties I was familiar with from my previous position. I was eventually able to secure a similar position as a manager of a group home in Brooklyn, New York. On this occasion, however, I was responsible for all aspects of running a facility for moderately and severely retarded adults with potentially serious behavior problems.

My next role came as I completed my Masters degree as a School Psychologist.  I was hired in that role for United Cerebral Palsy to evaluate and work with families of children and young adults with various degrees of physical and/or cognitive limitations.

With my experience and degree I was able to take the N.Y.S. test for Psychologist and later be called to work as a residential Psychologist at Suffolk Developmental Center. My assignment was to enable the residents to achieve their greatest potential in hopes of moving to a group home community setting.

My travels then found me in a comparable position, although on this occasion I was employed by Pilgrim Psychiatric Center using my therapeutic skills in working with chronic schizophrenic in-patients. My role as a member of a treatment team was to clinically stabilize each resident so that the resident might re-join society with family or in a supervised living facility. 

Within the  past 15 years I have been employed as a School Psychologist for BOCES on Long Island Specializing in assisting emotionally disturbed children, adolescents and their families in an academic setting.  In addition I am an adjunct Professor of Psychology at two colleges in Suffolk County. As a educator, psychologist and author of the above quotation, I feel the multitude of experiences I have had are beneficial in enlightening my students both within their social and academic environment.
