EDU 5419: Advanced Study in Organizational Theory

Spring 2009
Dr. Korynne Taylor-Dunlop

This course introduced by Dr. Dunlop as “problem based action research in design” allowed my fellow cohort members and me to “respond to the issues, review and further research in terms of her/his district, using theory from assigned texts, articles and all the doctoral courses to support problem solving”.  Each member of the cohort had the opportunity top present  modern organizational theory by integrating and presenting articles in the field that are seen today as classics. Utilizing the assigned textbook “Classics of Organizational Theory” by Shafritz, Ott and Jang (2005) all members of my cohort and me were able to present selected theorists to discuss and analyze in a group dialogue. We were then able to compare and contrast the different theorists we reviewed to enhance our understanding of organizational theory and to utilize this knowledge in development of our dissertation ideas.

The second part of our course involved being broken up into two randomly assigned groups to begin our team presentations. Here, Dr. Dunlop asked each group to begin discussing and responding to a series of questions related to the role of school leadership and administrative issues. It was imperative that we supported our views with actual theoretical framework, not simply opinions. The assignment was divided into three categories:

  1. Fundamental Issues, (open ended with basic inquires)
  2. In Depth Review, (specific comments and ideas)
  3. Probing further, (paths for further research and exploration)

At the conclusion of this course students in my cohort and me were significantly enlightened into the various theoretical frameworks related to organizational theory and allowed us to gain valuable insight into the dynamics and approaches one would need to comprehend into become a future leader.
