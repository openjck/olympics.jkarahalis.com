EDU 5420: Politics of Education

Summer 2007
Dr. Frank Smith

This course introduced Cohort members for the first time to Dr Smith in only our second class. After welcoming Cohort members and providing a biography about his background, Dr. Smith directed us to the Deborah Stone book Policy Paradox, as the foundation for this course. The issue of how can schools form a community was presented. What followed was a journey through the writings of Deborah Stone as well as Clarence Stone’s writing on Civic Capacity, two theorists whose interpretation of political discourse had much to contribute to future educators.

Deborah Stone presented two main models of political thought: the market and the polis. While the market system is rational, scientific and calculative, the polis system is fueled by ideas and conflict existing within the commons. Ideas and conflict arise out of political discourse which is described as a configuration of forces to generate commitment through the use of language and symbols. Stone uses the cake illustration in explaining the concept of values as it relates to goals. The four main areas of values used to accomplish a task include the following:

  • Equity - who gets what, why and how
  • Efficiency - the most output for a given input
  • Security - the needs of citizens and responsibilities of government
  • Liberty - freedom to act as long as their choices do not harm others

Using the Stone model students we instructed to review and analyze the “Blue Ribbon Project” in the Memphis City School District for its political rhetoric.  Cohort members were grouped to prepare a systematic coded approach in judging the “voices” represented in the polis.  The Blue Ribbon Initiative controversy stemmed from the discrepancy between the goal and the status quo.  The Blue Ribbon Initiative plan represents the complexities present in the cycle of policy making. 

The culmination of the course was preparing a mini-dissertation that was a construction of a mechanism in order to judge the different “voices” represented in the polis.  Upon completion of the exercise Cohort members were unanimous in their enlightened awareness of the numerous factors involved in effective political decision making. 

Class Materials

  • Stone, C. N. (2003). Civic Capacity: What Why and Whence.  Presented for Institutions of Democracy : Public Education, Oxford University Press.
