EDU 5741: Economics & Financing of Education

Spring 2009
Dr. Kevin McGuire

This course involved the concept of school finance defined as “the distribution and use of money for the purpose of providing educational services and providing student achievement”, Odden and Piccus (2004). Dr. McGuire presented cohort members with the eye opening information that Public Education along with only Health Care amount to the largest expenditures in New York City. Another point discussed asked cohort members to ponder if greater expenditures necessarily result in greater results. The effect of property and income was contrasted and how it affects community school taxes.

Dr. McGuire was able to present to my cohort members and me how budgets needs to have a vision, a set of principles and a way to effectively distribute funds. The model of where money for N.Y.S. school budgets was discussed and the following were found to include:

  • Federal Government
  • Income Tax
  • Local Property Taxes
  • Grants
  • Interest
  • Tuition
  • Leasing School Property  

As a class assignment students were required to attend a district Budget meeting to gain first hand experience of how budget meetings are conducted. For many of my cohort and me it was our first experience with a budget meeting in action. For myself it was a unique opportunity to observe the dynamics of a school district budget meeting and the difficult decisions made in eliminating popular programs despite community opposition in many cases. 

Among the many resources our cohort was able to review one that seemed most relevant to me was the N.Y.S. Department of Educations’ critical strategies that indicates school improvement. It was found to include:

  1. Curriculum
  2. Instruction
  3. Professional development
  4. School Climate
  5. Standards for School Leaders
  6. Student Support Services
  7. Academic Interaction Services
  8. Assessment
  9. Support For School Improvement Efforts

Included in the information acquired from this course was a PowerPoint presentation from recent graduate Dr. Cynthia Curtis-Seniuk on “Developing a District–Wide Departmental Budget”.  The information was highly relevant and provided cohort members and me with a first hand knowledge of how budgets are developed and implemented in a typical school district.
