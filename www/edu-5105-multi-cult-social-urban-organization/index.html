EDU 5105: Multi Cult Social Urban Organization

Spring 2008                                                                                                   Dr. Korynne Taylor Dunlop                                                                                                     

This course explored the school wide concerns future educators need to comprehend in working in a multicultural environment.  Areas of emphasis included:

  • Cultural Differences
  • English as a Second Language families (ESL)
  • Lower economic families
  • Parents and children of Color
  • Single or Multiply Disabled children

The course also provided students to look at cultural diversity from the prospective of the aforementioned in regards to “fitting in” with peers of dissimilar backgrounds. To insure the greatest success the course provided students with an understanding of Education that not only is Multicultural but Social Reconstuctionist as well.  This would include but not be limited to:

  • Involving Students in decision making
  • Involving working class parents actively in the school
  • Actively connecting with parents of Color in the school
  • Engaging the community in school activities
  • Linking the school in local community action projects
  • Making sure staffing patterns reflect diverse groups
  • Avoid reinforcing stereotypes

Through the assigned reading materials, class discussions and films, the course gave the members of my Cohort an eye opening awareness of cultures we would certainly encounter on a daily basis as future Educational Leaders.
