Edu.7900 Qualitative Research In Curriculum

Summer 2008
Dr. Korynne Taylor-Dunlop

The purpose of this course was to develop an understanding of qualitative research methods and their application to educational research. Students examined the theoretical foundations of qualitative research. This was accomplished through field work assignment, readings and class discussion. In the Cohort field work assignment students selected a topic related to their interest and a possible dissertation interest and apply a qualitative design in reporting back on the findings. Initially using a Focus Group, interview questions were derived that centered on key responses. Through the assigned readings and qualitative research presented by Doctorial graduates of the program, students were able to apply concepts of Patterns, Themes and Discrepancies found in qualitative research. Students presented their findings in class and written assignment that included a discussion and analysis related to the results of the topic selected.

Included in the course were presentations and discussion of case Studies and Historical Dissertations and how they compare and contrast from typical qualitative research.
