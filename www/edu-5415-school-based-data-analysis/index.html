EDU 5415: School Based Data Analysis

Fall 2007
Dr. Jonathan T. Hughes

This course introduced the Cohort members to the principles of using technology and tools in the analysis and presentation of data. A main purpose of the course was to acquaint and construct future Educational leader’s capacity to use data to improve student achievement; A need becoming more important in present day academic environment.

Dr. Hughes provides students with resources that provided valuable data needed to objectively study among other issues school performance on State assignments by district, school and student performance. Students learned through direct observation of the data and graphic representations the ability to read and interpret the information revealed. Through careful analysis Cohort members were able to identify trends, prioritize needs and identify questions the data raised. In addition, students learned to filter and sort information enabling Cohort members to narrow the scope to vital facts and information. Included in the course was acquiring knowledge in using codes to create a map which then can be used to demonstrate areas of significant importance to stakeholders.

Through individual and group assignments Cohort members were able to select a school district and develop a matrix that describes information collected from a variety of sources and to comparatively examiner data from regional as well as local school districts.  Data available for review included:

  • Achievement
  • Instruction
  • Enrollment
  • Demographics
  • Budgets
  • Personnel
  • Educational indicators

When analysis and review of all pertinent data was collected, Cohort members presented their findings.  The culmination of the assignments was to choose an aspiration district from the information collected and to develop specific steps to move the previously selected district towards meeting the positive features of the aspiration district.
