EDU 5103: Educational Governance and Policy

Spring 2009
Dr. Kevin McGuire

This course began with exploring the differences between governance and leadership and decision making processes. Referencing the Jim Collins books “Good to Great” and “Build to Last” students were able to discover the Triad of Leadership where leadership involves balancing continuity and change. It became clear to my cohort members and me that all effective leadership includes character, knowledge and action.

In order to comprehend how the process works, Dr. McGuire presented the legal hierarchy in the development of educational policy beginning with the U.S. Constitution and ending with the local school boards. The differences between “policy” and “operations” were clearly explained and cohort members were challenged to find concrete ways to improve education.

Another important and enlightening aspect of this course required cohort members to attend a local school board meeting and to obtain that school board’s policy. Each of us in our cohort was able to attend and report back on the various agendas we witnessed. For many in my cohort, including me, it was the first experience of an actual school board meeting. During our discussion on the various board meetings and policies cohort members were able to analyze the differences between a resolution and a policy as it relates to school board decisions. On closer assessment of policy we were able to come to consensus that policy includes:

  1. Identifying needs
  2. Developing criteria for at risk students
  3. Mentoring  (teacher participation)
  4. Parental Involvement (partnerships for success)
  5. Community Involvement

The course concluded with an analysis and group discussion on the Jim Collins book and the “Good to Great” model of people, ideas and actions. Concepts such as “getting the right people on the bus”, “confronting the brutal facts” and the Hedgehog model enabled cohort members to comprehend the challenges future leaders must contend with.
