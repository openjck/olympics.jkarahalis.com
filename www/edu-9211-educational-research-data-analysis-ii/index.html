EDU 7211: Educational Research & Data Analysis II

Fall 2008
Dr. Nicholas Bernice

This course continued the study of statistical inquiry that began in EDU. 5655. Areas addressed included more advanced univariate statistical methods, principles of measurement, power analysis and effect size. Areas of study included:

  • Descriptive and Inferential Statistics
  • Computing and understanding Averages
  • Variability
  • Frequency Distributions
  • Correlations
  • Reliability and Validity
  • Research Hypothesis
  • Normal Distribution
  • Statistical Significance
  • t-tests
  • ANOVA

Students were instructed to apply the statistical measures in a number of exercises using SPSS outputs in demonstration the competency of the above techniques. Exercises were presented in both word and PowerPoint and cohort review and recommendations were offered. The final assignment involved a paper reviewing four peer reviewed journal articles on an area of study we were considering for a possible Dissertation topic. The assignments allowed members of the Cohort to gain important necessary experience in applying statistical measures and applications to be used in future research.

Textbook

  • Salkind, N. J. (2005) Statistics for People Who Think They Hate Statistics (2nd. Ed.).  Thousand Oaks, CA: Sage
