EDU 5571: Administrative Leadership in Schools

Fall 2007
Dr. Frank Smith

This course involved examining framing school leadership through various schools of thought. Included were:

  • Deborah Stone (social capital change and politics)
  • Clarence Stone (Civic Capacity)
  • Dr. Vincent L. Ferrandino (A New Day for Learning)
  • Richard Florida (Tolerance, Talent and Technology)

Utilizing concepts from Deborah Stones book and knowledge acquired from previous class with Dr. Smith, (EDU 5420), Cohort members were able re-visit concepts and compare and contrast them with present readings on Civic Capacity. Cohort members carefully reviewed four cited school systems, (Kent County, Boston, Philadelphia and El Paso). Elements that make up the various concepts were identified. 

An additional assignment had Cohort members answering a series of questions about “Elmtown”, a Mill town, solely from a map. Cohort members analyzed the social, economic, and academic history of Elmtown utilizing knowledge from previous readings of Deborah Stone and Clarence Stone’s work.  It was agreed that as future school leaders Cohort members need to remember the following questions in choosing the models of school reform:

  • Does the model engage in the development of social capital?
  • Does the model require the engagement of civic capacity?
  • Does the model consider the education of all children?
  • Does it incorporate technology?
  • Does it engage in community building?

The course enabled Cohort members to continue to bring resolution to conflict using the various frameworks studied.

Textbook

  • Stone, D. (2002). Policy Paradox: The Art of Political Decision Making (Rev .ed.). New York, NY: W.W. Norton & Company.
