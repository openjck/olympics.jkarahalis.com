EDU 5655: Educational Research & Data Analysis I

Fall 2008
Dr. Jonathan T. Hughes

This course introduced the students to the SPSS 10 statistical program utilized in data based analysis research. Although primarily considered for quantitative research, the relevance of SPSS in qualitative research was also explored. Utilizing information from research data, (Urbana Schools) students were instructed to apply the principles in SPSS under frequencies and descriptives and to look for any patterns, themes or discrepancies between students, parents, teachers and the community in responses presentedfrom the survey. Working in Cohort groups, students were instructed to build a Matrix and prepare a PowerPoint presentation as if they were consultants hired by the schools board to evaluate the strengths and weakness of the Urbana schools. In addition, based on the results from the SPSS analysis, specific recommendations were formulated to be presented to the board that were realistic and reflected the conclusions drawn from the statistical conclusions.

The exercises presented in this course gave valuable practical experience in learning to navigate SPSS in applying specific data in assessment of raw data into relevant concrete conclusions that will be necessary not only for Dissertation applications but as future academic leaders.
