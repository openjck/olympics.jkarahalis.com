Edu.7800 - Advanced Research Design

Summer 2008
Dr. Frank Smith

This course prepared students for understanding and implementing a Dissertation proposal. The comparisons and differences between a Qualitative and Quantitative research design were evaluated.  Students were asked to explore research topic of interest and discern if such topic was qualitative of quantitative in possible design. When the appropriate approach was selected, students, with Dr. Smith’s guidance were able to formulate a problem centered on the topic of interest. The analogy of using a “road map” in organizing the research from problem to literature review, methods possible results and conclusions mirrored the format necessary in creating a valid dissertational proposal.

Students were able to review, comment and critique dissertation proposals from other students and peers and discuss alternative methodologies from the topics presented.

In addition students were given exposure to the availability of computer programs (I.e.) NVivo in computing quantitative research.

Class assignment involved deciding on and presenting the qualitative and/or quantitative approach to be utilized, coding system and to build a mini-dissertation using the concepts necessary in the selected design.
